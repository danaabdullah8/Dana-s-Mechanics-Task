# 🐕 NOVA Robot Dog

## 📌 Overview

NOVA Robot Dog is a simple quadruped robot designed to demonstrate basic mechanical engineering concepts, including chassis design, leg movement, stability, and torque calculation. The project was designed using Onshape as part of a mechanical engineering assignment.

---

## ✨ Features

- Four-legged robot design
- Lightweight rectangular chassis
- Articulated leg mechanism
- Stable walking concept (Trot Gait)
- Modular mechanical structure
- Designed using Onshape

---

## ⚙️ Mechanical Specifications

### Body
- Lightweight rectangular chassis
- Space for battery and electronic components

### Legs
- Four articulated legs
- Upper and lower leg segments
- Rotational joints

### Degrees of Freedom (DOF)
- 2 DOF per leg
- Total: 8 DOF

### Motors
Recommended actuator:
- Servo Motor (MG996R or equivalent)

---

## 📐 Torque Calculation

Given:
- Leg Mass = 0.2 kg
- Leg Length = 0.10 m
- Gravity = 9.81 m/s²

Formula:

Torque = m × g × r

Calculation:

0.2 × 9.81 × 0.10 = 0.196 N·m

Recommended Servo Torque:
≥ 0.5 N·m

---

## ⚖️ Stability

The battery and major components are placed near the center of the chassis to improve:
- Balance
- Stability
- Weight distribution

---

## 🚶 Walking Method

The robot uses a Trot Gait, where diagonal legs move together to provide stable and efficient walking.

---

## ⚠️ Expected Challenges

- Joint wear
- Servo overheating
- Limited battery life
- Reduced stability on uneven surfaces
